Dec 13 | St. Lucia’s Day

This festival of lights celebrated by Norway, Sweden and parts of Finland commemorates the martyrdom of St. Lucia, who was killed by the Romans for her religious beliefs. Young girls dress up as St. Lucia in white dresses with red sashes, and crowns of lit candles on their heads.


Dec 16-24 | Las Posadas

The Spanish word for lodging, this nine-day celebration that takes place in Latin America and parts of the United States praises Mary and Joseph (parents of Jesus Christ) and their journey to Jerusalem. The nine days represents Mary’s nine-month pregnancy.


Dec 21 | Winter Solstice/ Yule

These Pagan rituals that began centuries ago in Europe start at sundown on the shortest day of the year, and symbolize rebirth and new beginnings.


Nov 28 - Dec 6 | Hanukkah (Chanukah)

The Jewish “Festival of Lights” lasts eight days and nights, and celebrates the Jewish victory over the Syrian Greeks that restored their independence. During the festival, one candle is lit on the menorah each night just after sunset.


Dec 26 – Jan 1, 2022 | Kwanzaa

Started in 1966 by Dr. Maulana Karenga to honor universal African-American culture and heritage, Kwanzaa was inspired by African harvest festivals. This seven-day celebration involves lighting candles in a kinara each day.


Dec 26 | Boxing Day

Beginning in England in the 1830s on the first work day after Christmas, this secular holiday is to thank postal workers, shopkeepers and servants who had to work while others were feasting and opening presents. Traditionally, they receive their own “Christmas Box” of small gifts and treats.


Jan 10, 2022 | Bodhi Day

The Buddhist holiday honors the exact moment of Buddha’s awakening. A ficus tree decorated with multi-colored strings of lights symbolizes the various paths to Nirvana.
